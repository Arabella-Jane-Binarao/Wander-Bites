Discover Mexico


History & Landmarks

Mexico boasts numerous historical sites and architectural wonders:

  • Mexico City: The capital city features landmarks such as the Zócalo, the Metropolitan Cathedral, and the ancient Aztec temple Templo Mayor. Nearby, the pyramids of Teotihuacan are a must-see.
  • Chichen Itza: One of the New Seven Wonders of the World, this ancient Mayan city on the Yucatán Peninsula is famous for its pyramid, El Castillo.
  • Puebla: Known for its colonial architecture, the city is home to beautiful churches and the stunning Puebla Cathedral.

Art & Culture

Mexico has a rich cultural scene influenced by indigenous and Spanish heritage:

  • Museums and Galleries: The National Museum of Anthropology in Mexico City, the Frida Kahlo Museum (La Casa Azul), and the Museum of Modern Art.
  • Music and Festivals: Traditional mariachi music, the Day of the Dead (Día de los Muertos) celebrations, and the Guelaguetza festival in Oaxaca.
  • Theatre and Dance: Ballet Folklórico de México and performances at the Palacio de Bellas Artes.

Natural Beauty

Mexico's landscapes are varied and breathtaking:

  • Beaches: Cancun, Playa del Carmen, and Tulum on the Caribbean coast, and Cabo San Lucas and Puerto Vallarta on the Pacific coast.
  • Cenotes: Natural sinkholes found in the Yucatán Peninsula, perfect for swimming and diving.
  • Mountains and Volcanoes: The Sierra Madre mountain ranges, Pico de Orizaba, and Popocatépetl.

Cuisine & Drinks

Mexican cuisine is world-renowned and incredibly diverse:

  • Regional Specialties: Tacos, tamales, mole (a rich sauce from Oaxaca), and cochinita pibil (slow-roasted pork from Yucatán).
  • Drinks: Tequila and mezcal, traditional Mexican beers, and aguas frescas (fruit-based drinks).

Shopping

Mexico offers a variety of shopping experiences:

  • Mexico City: Upscale boutiques in Polanco, traditional markets like La Merced, and artisanal crafts in Coyoacán.
  • Oaxaca: Known for its vibrant markets selling textiles, pottery, and handmade crafts.
  • Guadalajara: Famous for its leather goods and the Mercado Libertad, one of the largest markets in Latin America.

Outdoor Activities

Mexico provides numerous opportunities for outdoor adventures:

  • Water Activities: Snorkeling and diving in Cozumel, whale watching in Baja California, and surfing in Sayulita.
  • Hiking and Climbing: The Copper Canyon (Barranca del Cobre), the ancient ruins of Palenque, and the volcanic peaks.
  • Ecotourism: Visiting biosphere reserves like Sian Ka'an and exploring the Lacandon Jungle.

Accommodation

Mexico offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets:

  • Luxury Resorts: Especially popular in destinations like Cancun, Riviera Maya, and Los Cabos.
  • Boutique Hotels: Charming, smaller hotels in cities like San Miguel de Allende and Oaxaca.
  • Budget Options: Hostels, guesthouses, and eco-lodges in various regions.

Practical Information

  • Language: Spanish is the official language, but English is widely spoken in tourist areas.
  • Currency: Mexican Peso (MXN).
  • Transportation: Efficient public transportation in cities (metro, buses), long-distance buses, and domestic flights for traveling between regions.

Travel Tips

  • Best Time to Visit: November to April for pleasant weather, avoiding the rainy season and hurricane season on the coasts.
  • Safety: Mexico is generally safe for tourists, but it’s important to stay informed about local advisories and avoid certain areas.
  • Etiquette: Greetings are warm and friendly, often involving a handshake or a kiss on the cheek. It’s courteous to use basic Spanish phrases.
